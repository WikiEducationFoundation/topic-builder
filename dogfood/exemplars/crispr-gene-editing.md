# Menu card

**Shape axes**

- structural: technical discipline with applications + ethics
- scale: hundreds (~100–250)
- layered_shape: core+periphery — technical core + variants /
  applications / ethics layers
- non-Anglosphere depth: low — recent and English-dominant in
  primary sources, though Asian regulatory contexts matter for
  ethics/applications
- biography density: medium — researchers (including Nobel laureates),
  ethicists, regulatory figures
- canonical category coverage: medium — `Category:CRISPR` exists but
  isn't deeply nested; reach requires WikiProject + navbox routes
- recall_ceiling_driver: substrate dispersal under parent canopy —
  most relevant articles live under broader genetic-engineering
  / molecular-biology framing, not under the topic's own category

**Doesn't apply when:** the discipline is mature with deep category
nesting (category-tree pulls do most of the work); OR WikiProject
coverage is weak; OR there's no clear parent technical canopy.

**Shape (prose).** A technical scientific discipline that emerged
recently (post-2012), with a tight technical core (Cas proteins,
guide RNA, repair mechanisms) and a fast-growing periphery of variants
(base editing, prime editing), applications (therapeutics,
agriculture, gene drives), and ethics / regulation. Recall is
substrate-driven — most of the topic-relevant articles exist on
Wikipedia under the broader genetic-engineering canopy, not under
CRISPR's own category.

**Summary.** Best run reached ~150 articles at ~98% precision and ~75%
recall by anchoring on `Category:CRISPR`, then expanding via
`harvest_navbox` on the parent genetic-engineering template, plus
WikiProject Genetics article lists for the broader biological context.
Applications and ethics layers pulled cleanly from list pages. Cross-
language walks added little — coverage is genuinely English-dominant
for this recent topic.

**High-leverage moves**:

- **`harvest_navbox` on the parent genetic-engineering template**
  surfaces the broader technical context (Cas variants, related
  techniques, foundational biology) that CRISPR's own category misses.
- **WikiProject Genetics / Molecular Biology article lists** are
  high-quality recall sources — recent technical disciplines
  benefit more from WikiProject coverage than from category trees.
- **`P101` (field of work)** Wikidata probe for biographies — pulls
  researchers whose field includes CRISPR / gene editing without
  requiring them to be in CRISPR-specific categories.
