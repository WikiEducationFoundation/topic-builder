# Scope: CRISPR gene editing

Frozen: 2026-04-23. Revisit deliberately — a scope change invalidates
the gold audit.

## Short statement

Wikipedia articles about CRISPR as a gene-editing system — its biology,
mechanisms, associated techniques and tools, pioneering scientists,
CRISPR-focused companies, applications (therapies and projects), and
the central bioethical episode (He Jiankui affair).

## In scope

- **CRISPR biology and mechanisms.** The CRISPR main article, CRISPR
  RNA, Cas-family proteins (Cas9, Cas12a, Cas3), guide RNA,
  protospacer adjacent motif (PAM), tracrRNA / crRNA, anti-CRISPR
  proteins, CRISPR-associated transposons (CASTs).
- **CRISPR editing techniques.** Prime editing, base editing,
  CRISPR activation (CRISPRa), CRISPR interference (CRISPRi),
  CRISPR-Display, CRISPR/Cas tools, genome-wide CRISPR-Cas9 knockout
  screens, epigenome editing via CRISPR, LEAPER, TIGR-Tas, off-target
  editing, GUIDE-Seq.
- **Foundational scientists.** Jennifer Doudna, Emmanuelle Charpentier,
  Feng Zhang, Virginijus Šikšnys, Rodolphe Barrangou, Francisco
  Mojica, Yoshizumi Ishino, Luciano Marraffini, Erik J. Sontheimer,
  J. Keith Joung, Samuel H. Sternberg, Lei Stanley Qi, Patrick Hsu.
- **CRISPR-focused companies.** CRISPR Therapeutics, Editas Medicine,
  Intellia Therapeutics, Mammoth Biosciences, Innovative Genomics
  Institute.
- **Approved / clinical-trial therapies.** Exagamglogene autotemcel
  (Casgevy), Verve PCSK9-inhibitor gene therapy, and specific patients
  whose cases are central to the CRISPR clinical narrative (Victoria
  Gray — first sickle-cell CRISPR patient; KJ Muldoon — early N=1
  gene therapy).
- **He Jiankui affair.** He Jiankui, the He Jiankui affair article,
  Designer baby, Human germline engineering, closely-affiliated
  Chinese researchers when the article documents their CRISPR-
  specific involvement.

## Explicitly out of scope

- **Generic biotech / technology noise.** Cement, Plastic, Submarine,
  Vehicle, Transport, Technology, Integrated circuit, Incandescent
  light bulb, Non-fungible token, Umeå, Sputnik crisis. These are
  lexical-search noise and should never have entered the corpus.
- **Broad list articles.** "List of inventions and discoveries by
  women" — mentions CRISPR but isn't about it.
- **Tangential cultural works.** "Fully Automated Luxury Communism"
  touches CRISPR in one chapter among many; OUT.
- **Unrelated VC / finance firms.** Ark Invest is not a CRISPR-focused
  entity; OUT. (Flagship Pioneering founded Editas and IS materially
  part of the CRISPR venture-capital story — PERIPHERAL, not OUT.)
- **Generic disease articles where CRISPR is one of many angles.**
  Thalassemia as a broad umbrella → OUT. (Beta thalassemia, which is
  the specific CRISPR therapy target, is PERIPHERAL.)

## Ambiguity rulings

- **Pioneer vs. CRISPR-user biographies.** A scientist is CENTRAL if
  they're credited for a foundational contribution (Cas9 repurposing,
  PAM discovery, early CRISPR mechanism work, prime/base editing
  invention, pioneering clinical trials). A scientist who merely uses
  CRISPR in their research is PERIPHERAL at most. When in doubt,
  check whether The Code Breaker (Isaacson) or similar CRISPR
  histories feature them.
- **Chinese researchers associated with He Jiankui.** The He Jiankui
  affair article and He Jiankui himself are CENTRAL. Chen Hu, Deng
  Hongkui, and similar figures whose articles mention He Jiankui
  context but whose notability is broader stem-cell work → PERIPHERAL
  or OUT depending on CRISPR specificity. Default PERIPHERAL when
  unclear.
- **Disease targets.** Sickle cell disease (primary CRISPR therapy
  target) → PERIPHERAL. Beta thalassemia → PERIPHERAL. Generic
  "Thalassemia" umbrella → OUT.
- **"Genome editing" as a field article.** CENTRAL — it's the parent
  technique, and CRISPR is its dominant implementation.
- **"Gene therapy" article.** PERIPHERAL — broader category than CRISPR.
- **Gene knockout / knock-in / knockdown / targeting.** PERIPHERAL —
  related molecular-biology techniques; not CRISPR-specific but
  ubiquitous in CRISPR literature.
- **University of California, Berkeley.** PERIPHERAL because it's
  Doudna's institution and central to the CRISPR story; borderline
  with OUT if interpreted strictly.
- **Science-popularization books.** *The Code Breaker* (Isaacson,
  about Doudna) → PERIPHERAL. *Fully Automated Luxury Communism*
  (one-chapter touch) → OUT.
- **Documentary films.** *Human Nature* (2019) is a CRISPR documentary
  → PERIPHERAL. *Make People Better* (2022) about He Jiankui →
  PERIPHERAL.
- **Application projects.** Colossal Biosciences' dire wolf project,
  woolly mouse: specific CRISPR applications → PERIPHERAL.
- **Generic related terms.** RNA editing, Recombinant DNA, Small
  nucleolar RNA — adjacent molecular biology → PERIPHERAL.

## Known scope revisits / open questions

- **Where exactly does "gene therapy pioneer" end and "CRISPR
  pioneer" begin?** Kiran Musunuru (CRISPR gene therapy for heart
  disease) is PERIPHERAL or CENTRAL depending on how tightly we
  read. Default CENTRAL because his work is CRISPR-delivery-focused.
- **Tangential biotech entrepreneurs.** Sean Parker (philanthropic
  funder of CRISPR cancer immunotherapy) → PERIPHERAL. Janice Chen
  (Mammoth Biosciences co-founder) → CENTRAL because Mammoth is a
  CRISPR company.

## Cross-wiki relevance

English Wikipedia is primary. CRISPR articles have reasonable coverage
on Chinese / Japanese wikis given the He Jiankui affair and Ishino's
seminal work; a cross-wiki pass would likely add a handful (~5–15)
translation candidates. Medium priority for reach.
