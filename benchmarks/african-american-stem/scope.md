# Scope: African American people in STEM

Frozen: 2026-04-23. Revisit deliberately — a scope change invalidates
the gold audit.

Modeled after the 2026-04-17-frozen hispanic-latino-stem-us scope
with heritage swapped to African American / Black American.

## Short statement

Wikipedia biographies of people of African American / Black American
heritage, working in STEM research fields, with meaningful US
affiliation.

## In scope

- **Heritage.** African American / Black American. "Black American",
  African diaspora people whose notability includes US-affiliated
  work. Black immigrants who built a US research career (Nigerian-
  American, Ghanaian-American, Kenyan-American, Cuban-American of
  Afro-Latino heritage, etc.) are IN. Black British / Caribbean-born
  scientists with substantive US research work are IN.
- **STEM fields.** Natural sciences (physics, chemistry, biology,
  astronomy, geology, meteorology, oceanography, ecology, neuroscience,
  environmental science, paleontology, hydrogeology, soil science),
  mathematics and statistics, engineering (including biomedical and
  aerospace), computer science, astronautics (astronauts included),
  inventors / technologists, materials science, nanoscience,
  biochemistry, microbiology, genetics, physiology, pharmacology.
- **US affiliation.** US-born counts. Immigrants who pursued a STEM
  career at US institutions count. Foreign researchers with a
  substantive postdoc or research position in the US then returning
  home count. The bar is "some real US-based research activity,"
  not "lifelong US resident."

## Explicitly out of scope

- **Clinical medicine.** Physicians whose notability is primarily
  clinical practice, medical administration, authorship of popular
  books, or medical education are OUT. **Physician-scientists whose
  notability is primarily research (lab work, clinical research
  programs, publication record) are IN,** including MDs and MD/PhDs.
  Biomedical engineers are IN.
- **Social sciences.** Economists, psychologists, sociologists,
  anthropologists, political scientists — OUT, even if they use
  quantitative / statistical methods.
- **STEM education (without research).** STEM educators whose
  notability is teaching, curriculum design, advocacy, or
  administration rather than research — OUT. STEM researchers
  who also teach — IN.
- **No US affiliation.** Black African / Caribbean scientists with
  no substantive US research ties — OUT.
- **Non-science roles.** Politicians, lawyers, athletes, musicians,
  artists, actors, journalists, poets, military officers (without
  STEM research work), religious leaders, business executives
  (without STEM-primary notability), activists, fraternity founders.
- **Architecture.** Architects (licensed architecture practitioners)
  are OUT. Civil / structural / environmental engineers are IN.

## Ambiguity rulings (default decisions)

- **Physician-scientists** (MD or MD/PhDs doing primarily research).
  IN. Same rule as hispanic-latino-stem-us scope.
- **Astronauts.** IN (astronautics explicitly listed as STEM).
- **STEM + secondary activism / politics / entrepreneurship.** IN
  when STEM is the primary profession listed. Out when STEM role
  appears only in passing.
- **Entrepreneurs with STEM credentials.** IN if their notability
  is rooted in engineering / invention / research. OUT if primarily
  a businessperson whose company happens to be in tech.
- **Popular-science writers with PhDs.** IN if they have an active
  research career; OUT if their Wikipedia notability is as a writer.
- **Academic (bare, no field).** PERIPHERAL default — the source
  implies STEM but the description doesn't confirm. Case-by-case
  if a future run surfaces more detail.
- **Athletes who are also STEM researchers.** Meredith Gourdine is
  a known edge — long jumper + engineer. IN via the STEM qualifier.

## Cross-reference: AA STEM medicine blocklist

The "AA STEM medicine blocklist" topic (id 8, 807 articles) was
built to separately track candidates for clinical-medicine exclusion.
Articles in that list are treated as prima facie OUT by the audit
classifier unless the description includes a research qualifier
(researcher, scientist, laboratory, professor, etc.) that flips
the case to IN per the physician-scientist rule.

## Known scope revisits / open questions

- **Edge cases around "research enough to count."** Borderline is a
  principal investigator with 3+ publications vs. a clinical
  administrator with one review paper. Default: principal
  investigators IN, administrators OUT.
- **STEM people who later became politicians / executives.** Their
  notability should be assessed on research career if that's
  substantive, regardless of later career pivot.
- **"Scholar of education"** — OUT. The phrase captures education-
  research which falls outside natural-sciences / engineering scope.
