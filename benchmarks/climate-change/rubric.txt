# Centrality rubric — climate-change benchmark
# Frozen 2026-04-25. A rubric change invalidates gold.csv and requires re-audit.

CENTRAL — Articles directly about climate change / global warming and
  its science, attribution, modeling, and projections; the IPCC and its
  reports; named international climate agreements, conferences, and
  frameworks (UNFCCC, Kyoto, Paris, every annual COP); the global
  climate movement, named protests, and climate-policy campaigns;
  climate impacts and effects (sea level rise, species range shifts,
  climate-driven migration, climate-attributed extreme weather events,
  ice sheet loss); climate-tipping-points and feedback mechanisms;
  climate scientists, activists, and policy figures whose primary
  notability is climate work; major climate NGOs / advocacy orgs /
  governmental bodies whose mission is climate; "Climate change in
  [country/region]" articles; named, climate-attributed extreme
  weather events; annual "[year] in climate change" summaries.

PERIPHERAL — Mitigation technologies and energy systems whose article
  frames them in climate-response terms (renewable energy, solar /
  wind / hydro, EVs, carbon capture and storage, nuclear power as
  low-carbon, energy efficiency, green building, carbon markets,
  ESG); paleoclimate, atmospheric science, and oceanography articles
  whose subject is the climate-relevant mechanism (greenhouse-gas
  chemistry at the aggregate level, methane clathrates, ocean
  acidification, glacier hydrology, carbon cycle); deforestation and
  land-use change articles, including "Deforestation in [country]";
  biographies of scientists / activists / politicians whose climate
  work is significant but not their primary identity; cultural works
  about climate (films, novels, video games, art) when climate is the
  explicit subject; environmental movements and orgs whose scope
  includes climate; geographic / national energy-transition articles
  ("Energy in [country]" when framed around decarbonization);
  sustainability / circular-economy articles when the framing is
  environmental.

OUT — Petroleum geology, gas / oil industry processes that aren't
  climate-framed (drilling, refining, well-completion, pipeline
  operations); chemistry of methane / CO₂ / fluorocarbons unrelated
  to atmospheric / climate context (industrial processes, food
  chemistry, "carbonated" beverages, semiconductor uses, individual
  fluorocarbon refrigerant articles); generic meteorology and weather
  articles not framed as climate (everyday forecasting, generic storm
  taxonomy, individual non-attributed weather events); generic
  engineering articles about renewable-tech components when not
  climate-framed (specific turbine bearings, panel-manufacturing
  techniques); generic motor-vehicle articles even if low-emission;
  biographies of energy-industry figures whose work is not climate-
  centric; works of fiction with only incidental climate themes;
  "X in popular culture" pages that mostly enumerate fiction;
  generic country / capital-city articles surfaced via "by country"
  list-page harvests; "Geography of [country]" articles.

# Notes for auditors:
# - Climate change is unusually well-organized on Wikipedia: a strong
#   WikiProject (4,453 articles tagged), a dense category tree (depth-3
#   safe pull = 2,933 articles), mature curated indexes (Index of
#   climate change articles, Glossary of climate change). The build
#   triangulates well — 32% multi-sourced in the 2026-04-25 baseline.
# - Source-trust is reasonable on `wikiproject:Climate change` and on
#   `category:Climate change` at depth ≤3 with chemistry-drift branches
#   pruned (Methane / Greenhouse gases / Carbon dioxide / Sustainable
#   energy excluded). Articles only-from-`list_page:Outline of solar
#   energy` need stricter review — the source surfaces engineering /
#   biographical noise alongside genuine periphery.
# - The Toyota-vehicles pattern and the "Geography of [country]"
#   pattern were both pruned in the baseline run; an audit classifier
#   should re-block those pre-commit if it sees them re-appear.
# - Climate activist (P106=Q61048378) Wikidata sitelinks are noisy:
#   poets, actors, and celebrities-with-climate-causes are tagged with
#   that property. Default PERIPHERAL when title-only, CENTRAL when the
#   shortdesc explicitly identifies climate-activism as the primary
#   role.
