# Scope: Apollo 11

Frozen: 2026-04-23. Revisit deliberately — a scope change invalidates the
gold audit and requires a re-run.

## Short statement

Wikipedia articles whose primary subject is, or is directly a part of,
the Apollo 11 crewed lunar-landing mission of July 1969 — its crew,
spacecraft, landing site, science program, cultural legacy, and things
officially named after it.

## In scope

- **The mission itself.** Apollo 11, its launch, flight path, landing,
  EVA, return, and splashdown.
- **Crew.** Neil Armstrong, Buzz Aldrin, Michael Collins — their full
  biographies.
- **Ground support and mission control.** Gene Kranz, Christopher Kraft,
  Steve Bales, Charles Duke (CAPCOM), other named controllers and
  flight directors whose role in 11 is documented.
- **Spacecraft and hardware used by 11 specifically.** Columbia (CSM-107),
  Eagle (LM-5), Saturn V SA-506. Ancillary hardware and experiments
  deployed on 11 (EASEP package components, Passive Seismic Experiment,
  Laser Ranging Retroreflector, Early Apollo Scientific Experiments
  Package).
- **Landing site.** Tranquility Base, Mare Tranquillitatis context
  directly relevant to the landing.
- **Primary cultural works about 11.** Films (*First Man*, *Apollo 11*
  (2019 doc), *Moonwalk One*), books (*Of a Fire on the Moon*,
  *Carrying the Fire*, *First Man* (Hansen), *Chariots for Apollo*
  — the NASA SP-4205 history), plays, songs whose main subject is 11.
- **Things named after Apollo 11.** Schools, streets, lunar craters
  (Armstrong, Aldrin, Collins craters), US vessels, institutions.
- **Artifacts and memorabilia of 11.** Goodwill messages, lunar samples
  from the mission, landed hardware, display artifacts.
- **Adjacent Apollo missions whose content materially overlaps 11.**
  Apollo 8, 10, 12 as PERIPHERAL members (trajectory rehearsal,
  preparatory tests, immediate successor).

## Explicitly out of scope

- **Non-Apollo Moon programs.** Luna (Soviet), Chang'e (Chinese),
  Surveyor (US uncrewed), Chandrayaan (Indian), Pioneer, Ranger.
  These are OUT even though they share subject matter.
- **General spaceflight history.** "History of spaceflight",
  "Timeline of space exploration", "Space Race" as a whole. OUT when
  they mention 11 but aren't primarily about it.
- **Generic Moon / astronomy articles.** "Moon", "Selenology",
  "Lunar geology" general articles — OUT. Narrower geology articles
  SPECIFIC to the 11 landing site (e.g. "Mare Tranquillitatis" only
  if the article's main content is the landing site) are borderline —
  default PERIPHERAL.
- **Conspiracy-theory articles.** Articles whose main subject is
  moon-landing conspiracy discourse (e.g. "Moon landing conspiracy
  theories") are OUT. Specific films or books that ARE conspiracy
  works and that Wikipedia describes as such: default OUT unless
  the article's focus is Apollo 11's cultural impact via the
  conspiracy.
- **Broad Apollo program articles.** "Apollo program", "Project Apollo",
  "Apollo astronaut" — these are foundational context but OUT because
  their subject is the program, not 11 specifically. Individual later
  Apollo missions (13–17) are OUT.
- **People tangentially connected.** Astronauts who didn't fly 11
  and had no specific 11 role (most Gemini astronauts, most later
  Apollo astronauts). OUT unless they're mission-control personnel
  with a documented 11 role.
- **Apollo hardware shared across missions.** "Saturn V", "Apollo
  Command/Service Module" as classes — PERIPHERAL (they contextualize
  11's hardware). Default keep in corpus but score PERIPHERAL.

## Ambiguity rulings (default decisions)

- **Crew biographies** are CENTRAL — the person IS the mission.
- **Launch site articles** (Kennedy Space Center Launch Complex 39A) —
  CENTRAL. LC-39 as the launch facility, KSC itself as the support
  infrastructure (the KSC main article IS ambiguous — default
  PERIPHERAL since most of KSC isn't about Apollo 11 specifically).
- **Lunar geology / crater articles named after 11 crew** (Armstrong
  crater, Collins crater) — CENTRAL (named-after rule).
- **Cultural works mentioning 11 among other subjects** (e.g. a
  novel that features an Apollo-11 scene) — OUT unless Wikipedia's
  coverage of the work is primarily about the 11 element.
- **"Apollo 11 in popular culture"** (the Wikipedia article itself) —
  IN as a topical index article (CENTRAL-for-the-list-page sense).
- **Post-mission artifacts on display** (Eagle's ascent stage if it
  existed, Columbia on display at NASM) — CENTRAL.
- **Goodwill messages microdisk** — CENTRAL (physical 11 artifact).

## Known scope revisits / open questions

- **Apollo 11 reunions / anniversary events.** Default PERIPHERAL;
  escalate if a later run adds many.
- **Post-mission honors of crew** (medals, ceremonial events). If
  the article is primarily about the honor, default PERIPHERAL.
  If it's about the honoree generally (biography), already CENTRAL.
- **Mission-control personnel whose Apollo 11 role is partial.**
  Case-by-case; default IN if documented at a CAPCOM / flight-director
  level, PERIPHERAL if support staff.

## Cross-wiki relevance

English Wikipedia is the primary build for this benchmark. Apollo 11
has strong coverage on enwiki; non-en wikis add relatively few unique
articles. Cross-wiki expansion is a lower-priority reach target for
this topic than it is for orchids.
